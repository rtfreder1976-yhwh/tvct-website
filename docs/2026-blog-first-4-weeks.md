What are the most commonly missed areas during spring cleaning? Throughout a standard spring clean, homeowners frequently miss high-dust zones like ceiling fan blades, air vents, the tops of kitchen cabinets, deep inside window tracks, and behind major appliances. ## The Top 5 Hidden Areas You're Probably Missing

### 1. Ceiling Fan Blades and Light Fixtures
**What Is Ceiling Fan Dusting?**
**Ceiling fan dusting** is the process of removing accumulated dust from the top surface and leading edges of fan blades to prevent allergens from circulating through the room when the fan is turned on.

If you turn on your fan and immediately sneeze, it's time for a deep clean. We utilize specialized extenders and microfiber traps to remove this dust rather than just knocking it onto your furniture.

### 2. Deep Inside Window Tracks
Window tracks accumulate dead bugs, dirt, and moisture that can lead to mold. It takes a specialized approach using precision brushes and vacuums to truly clear out these tight channels.

### 3. Above the Kitchen Cabinets
Out of sight usually means out of mind. The space above your kitchen cabinets collects a thick layer of sticky, greasy dust that standard dusters can't handle. It requires professional degreasing solutions to break down the grime.

### 4. Air Return Vents and Baseboards
Your HVAC system continuously pulls dust toward your air return vents. Over months, this buildup restricts airflow and covers your baseboards in a thick layer of fine particles. Hand-wiping baseboards is a core component of a professional deep clean.

### 5. Behind and Under Major Appliances
Crumbs, grease, and debris frequently fall beside the stove and under the refrigerator. Pulling these appliances out (when safe to do so) to sanitize the floors and walls behind them is critical for a sanitary kitchen.
