## One-line voice summary
Warm, steady, and quietly premium — a faith-grounded, veteran-disciplined neighbor who shows up exactly when promised and treats your home like it matters.

## Voice pillars

### 1. Steady, not hyped
We sound calm and certain — the kind of business that keeps its word. We don't shout, exclaim, or stack adjectives.
- We sound like: "We'll be there Tuesday at 9. Same team. Same checklist."
- Not like: "AMAZING DEEP CLEAN!! Book NOW before slots VANISH!!"

### 2. Disciplined warmth
Military precision meets a husband-and-wife family business. Concrete numbers and process language ($2M insured, 49-point checklist, 1,047+ cleanings) are softened by genuine care for the people in the home.
- We sound like: "Background-checked, $2M insured, and we'll text you when we're on our way."
- Not like: "Our caring family of cleaning professionals goes above and beyond to bring sparkle and joy to your space."

### 3. Quietly premium
Luxury without bragging. We describe the surfaces, the routines, and the standard — and let the reader infer the price tier. We say "white-glove" and "estate" sparingly; we never say "the best."
- We sound like: "pH-neutral products on original hardwoods. Streak-free on golf-course windows."
- Not like: "Indulge in the ultimate luxury concierge cleaning experience reserved for the most discerning clients."

### 4. Plainly honest
Numbers, not vague claims. Prices have a "From" prefix. Hours are stated. Areas are listed. If we can't promise something, we don't.
- We sound like: "Deep cleans from $276. Free quote in 60 seconds. No credit card."
- Not like: "Affordable rates customized to your unique needs — call for pricing!"

### 5. Neighbor-first
We're local. We name the neighborhood, the church, the base, the road. We're not a franchise routing your call to a call center.
- We sound like: "From Twickenham antebellums to Hampton Cove golf estates — we know the surfaces."
- Not like: "Serving customers nationwide with our trusted franchise network."

## Sentence patterns

1. **Short, declarative, specific.** "Deep cleans start at $276. A 2,000 sq ft home takes 4–5 hours."
2. **Pair a number with a promise.** "$2 million in liability coverage. Bonded. Background-checked."
3. **Name the place, then the person.** "From Hampton Cove golf estates to Monte Sano mountain homes — we know what your floors need."
4. **Soft three-beat closer.** "Veteran-owned. Women-led. Community-trusted."
5. **Address the reader's friction directly.** "Don't see your area? Call 256-826-1100 — we may still serve you."

## How to adapt for channel

### Landing pages
Open with a specific promise + a number. Lead with the price ("From $X") and the time-to-quote (60 seconds). Stack trust signals as a quiet row, not a brag. Close every section with a single, plain CTA. Avoid more than one exclamation point on a page.

### Emails
Subject lines: plain, specific, second-person. "Your Tuesday cleaning is confirmed" beats "Get ready for sparkle!" Body: short paragraphs, named sender (Todd or Christen), one CTA. Sign off "— Todd & Christen" for owner emails; "— The Valley Clean Team" for ops.

### SMS / quote follow-up
First-name only, no salesy preamble. "Hi Sarah — this is Christen with The Valley Clean Team. Your quote for the Hampton Cove deep clean is $381. Want to book a Tuesday or Thursday this week?" Always include opt-out. Never use emojis beyond a sparing checkmark.

### Social
Lead with a photo (before/after, a hand-detailed baseboard, the team in front of a house) and let the work speak. Captions are short and concrete — name the neighborhood, the service, the size. Avoid trend-chasing. Reels feel more like "here's how we do it" than "look how busy we are."

### Service-page body copy
Top-down structure: what's included → what's not → what it costs → who it's for → how to book. Use room-by-room lists with checkmarks. Quote real prices with "From" prefixes. Add a "Why this matters" sentence after each major section so it's not just a feature dump.
