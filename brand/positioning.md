## Messaging do/don't

**Do:**
- Lead with time on the clock — "quote in under 2 hours, clean today" beats every chain in the market
- Pair every speed claim with a proof element (photo, insurance dollar amount, review count)
- Use specific numbers: $2M insured, 130+ 5-star reviews, 49 checkpoints, 2-hour quote, same-day arrival
- Stack the urgency CTAs above the fold: "Get Quote in 2 Hours" → "Text for Same-Day" → "Call Now"
- In Birmingham/Nashville, layer the estate-grade language ON TOP of the speed angle, not instead of it

**Don't:**
- Don't lead Huntsville or Shoals copy with "49-point checklist" — MaidPro owns that number locally
- Don't lead with "veteran + women-owned" in Huntsville/Shoals — collides with MaidPro's own woman-owned franchise
- Don't claim "best in [city]" without a data point attached — every competitor does, it converts no one
- Don't promise eco-only — eco is table stakes, not a differentiator, and over-claiming narrows the buyer pool
- Don't bury the time guarantee below pricing or reviews — it's the conversion lever; it goes above the fold
- Don't compete on hourly rate — Homeaglow/Handy will always undercut; pivot the comparison to "insured, same team, photo-documented, same day"
