# Newsletter Format Template — "The Valley Note"

## Why this format

Subscribers who finished a 7-email welcome arc and didn't book are in *passive consideration*. They need:
1. **Useful** content (not pitches) so they keep opening
2. **A reason** to remember TVCT exists when they DO need a cleaner
3. **One small, soft CTA** per email, never the lead

The Valley Note is a fortnightly, ~400-word email with a tight format. Reads in under 90 seconds.

## The format

```
Subject: [Specific noun-led title — no questions, no clickbait]
Preview: [One sentence describing what's in it. No "Don't miss this" energy.]

Hi {{first_name}},

[ONE PARAGRAPH — the lead]
A short observation, often about something seasonal (allergy weeks, listing
season, college move-out, holiday hosting) or something the team has been
noticing in homes lately. 2–4 sentences. Specific, not generic.

[ONE PARAGRAPH — the useful thing]
A short tip, a question to ask, a thing to look for in your own home, or a
behind-the-scenes from a recent clean. 3–6 sentences. This is the "I read this
because it was useful" payoff.

[ONE PARAGRAPH — local color, optional]
A neighborhood, a season, a specific local thing. Not always present. When
present, max 2 sentences. Keeps the brand feeling local instead of generic.

[ONE LINE — the soft CTA]
Always soft. Always one. Examples:
- "If your spring cleaning is on your mind, we have a couple of Tuesdays open in {{month}}."
- "If you've been thinking about going recurring, reply with your ZIP and I'll send a flat number back."
- "If a friend asked you who to call, we'd love to be on that list."

— Todd or Christen (alternate by edition)
The Valley Clean Team
```

## Voice rules

- **400 words max.** If it's longer, cut.
- **Zero exclamation points.** Ever.
- **One CTA, soft, near the bottom.** Not the lead.
- **Specific over abstract.** Names of neighborhoods, dates, products, problems. Never "the leading provider" or "industry-leading."
- **Sign-off alternates** Todd / Christen across editions. Pick by feel — Christen for warm/relational, Todd for tactical/operational.
- **Faith/family thread:** at most once per quarter, never as the headline.

## Subject-line patterns that work

- `"A note about [specific seasonal thing]"` — e.g., "A note about Tennessee Valley pollen and your hardwoods"
- `"The thing we noticed in [N] [neighborhood] homes last week"`
- `"What to do with your [room/item] this [season]"`
- `"Why most cleaners skip [specific thing] (and what it costs you)"`
- `"A short story from a [neighborhood] [home type]"`

## Subject lines that DO NOT work for this brand

- Anything with "?" — too aggressive for the voice
- Anything with "FREE" / "LIMITED TIME" / "DON'T MISS"
- Anything that sounds like a chain
- Anything with emoji
- "Hey [name]" or "[name], you'll want to see this"

## Two finished editions

- `01-2026-05-spring-pollen-hardwoods.md` — seasonal angle, allergy/pollen + hardwood care
- `02-2026-06-college-move-out-deposits.md` — May/June Huntsville/Nashville move-out wave

## Cadence to follow after these two

Every other Tuesday, 7:30 AM CT. Use the format above. Rotate themes:

- **Seasonal home-care** (e.g., spring pollen, summer pet hair, fall dust, winter dry-air)
- **Behind-the-scenes from a recent clean** (anonymized)
- **A tactical home-care tip** (a product, a routine, a question to ask)
- **A neighborhood spotlight** (something specific to one market)
- **An honest pricing/market observation** (industry transparency)

Stay away from "cleaning hacks" lists, "10 ways to..." headlines, and Pinterest-style how-to formats. Those don't fit this voice.
