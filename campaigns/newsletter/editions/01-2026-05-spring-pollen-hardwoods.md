# Edition 1: Spring Pollen + Hardwoods

## Email Copy

Hi {{first_name}},

The pollen this week has been the worst of the season so far. If you live anywhere from the Shoals to Mountain Brook, you've probably wiped down the same windowsill twice this week and watched yellow film settle right back on the floor an hour later.

Here's the thing about this particular pollen wave on hardwood: the standard household instinct is to grab whatever's under the sink and damp-mop the floor. Don't. Most of what's under the sink is too alkaline for sealed hardwood and will start to dull the finish over a season of regular use. The "shiny floors" look in real-estate photos is from new oil, not a strong cleaner.

What works on hardwoods this time of year: a dry microfiber pad first (picks up about 80% of pollen and dust), followed by a barely-damp pad with pH-neutral hardwood cleaner — Bona's Hardwood Floor Cleaner is the most accessible one for the rest of us, and it's what our team uses on every floor over $30/sqft. The trick is two passes — dry first, then damp — not one wet pass that smears the pollen back into the grain.

A small thing we've been doing lately on Hampton Cove and Twickenham deep cleans: wiping the windowsills last instead of first. Pollen settles on horizontal surfaces for 3–4 hours after the windows are open, so wiping them at the start of a clean means re-wiping at the end. Just inverting the order saves a pass.
