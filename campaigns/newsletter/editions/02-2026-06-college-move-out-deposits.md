Hi {{first_name}},

June 1 is the busiest move-out date of the year across our markets. UAH, Vanderbilt, and Alabama all push leases over at the end of May, and the realtor wave hits the same week. Last June we did 47 move-out cleans between the Shoals and Nashville. We have notes.

The thing that surprises most first-time renters at a walk-through: the landlord almost never fails a unit on dirt. They fail it on two specific things, and both are 30-minute fixes if you know to look.

**1. Inside the oven and the broiler drawer.** This is the single most common deposit deduction. Walk-through inspectors open the oven — it's automatic. If there's baked-on residue, that's $75–$200 off your deposit at most apartments, $300+ at higher-end rentals. The broiler drawer underneath is where it gets worse — most renters don't even know it pulls out. A degreaser, a 20-minute soak, and a scrubbing pad fixes both.

**2. Behind the toilet (between the tank and the wall).** Second most common. It's not a cleanliness check — it's the inspector reaching back to confirm there's no water damage or unreported leak. If it's dusty back there, it photographs poorly on the move-out report. A microfiber and a flashlight, 90 seconds per bathroom. Most renters never look back there in three years of living in the unit.

A third thing that gets people, less common but expensive: the windowsill tracks. The tracks themselves, not the glass. They collect dust and dead bugs, and a landlord can flag them as "not clean" even if every other surface in the home is spotless. Vacuum first, then a damp microfiber down the track.
