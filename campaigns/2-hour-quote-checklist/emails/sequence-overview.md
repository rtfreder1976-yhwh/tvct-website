## Voice + positioning notes

- Sign-off rotation: **Christen** on connection/story emails (1, 2, 4, 7); **Todd** on tactical/math emails (3, 5, 6). Matches voice-profile pattern of "Todd as the tinkerer, Christen as the relational lead."
- Faith/family thread surfaces gently in **email 2** ("running this business to the glory of God"), then never repeats. Per voice-profile: meaningful differentiator, but quarantine to one mention.
- Zero exclamation points across all 7 emails (matches "steady, not hyped").
- "Same team, every time" used 3 times across the sequence — never as the lead.
- 49-point checklist mentioned **once**, in email 6 only as a supporting line. Per positioning.md MaidPro collision flag: never lead with this.
- Veteran-owned / women-led mentioned **once each**, in the closing trust row of email 7. Same MaidPro guard.
- Price points used (live from site): $99 recurring / $175 deep / $225 move-in-out. Move-out story uses $425 (real-feeling specific number, not a published rate).

## Send-time logic

Tuesday + Thursday at 7:30 AM CT is the standard B2C cadence for service businesses in the South-East. This audience is:
- Working households (dual-career, parents) — checks email pre-work
- Local — all in CT (no timezone split needed)
- Service buyers — opens for "useful information," not "exciting news"

Avoid Monday (inbox clearing), avoid Friday after noon (mentally gone), avoid weekend sends except for urgency-driven offers.

## What to do after this sequence

Subscribers who didn't book by day 12 should drop into the standing **newsletter cadence** (1 email every 2 weeks, value-led, occasional soft offer). Build that next via `/newsletter` if needed.

Subscribers who booked should get the **post-purchase sequence** (welcome aboard, what to expect on clean day, post-clean photo follow-up, recurring upsell at clean #3). Build that via `/email-sequences` re-run when ready.

## A/B tests prioritized across the sequence

1. **Email 1 subject:** A ("The 2-Hour Quote Checklist (inside)") vs C ("here's the checklist + a heads-up") — sets the voice register for the whole list
2. **Email 6 subject:** A ("The actual cost of one-off cleans (do the math)") vs B ("$3,120 vs $2,184 — same year, same house") — tells you how much "math marketing" the audience tolerates
3. **Email 7 subject:** A ("Last note about cleaning, then I'll stop") vs C ("quick question and then back to regular emails") — informs how to phrase ongoing newsletter close-outs
