# Email 4: Value Story

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "What happened on a Hampton Cove move-out" — recommended
Story hook + specific neighborhood. Curiosity-led but grounded — feels like a real story, not clickbait. Hampton Cove is recognizable to Huntsville readers; works fine for non-locals as a generic place name.

### B: "She'd called 6 cleaners. None of them showed."
Pattern interrupt opening with stakes. Higher open, slightly higher unsubscribe — some readers will assume it's a complaint thread.

### C: "the deposit walk-through was Monday morning"
Pure scene-setting, lowercase, no "I" or "you." Reads like the start of a story someone is telling at dinner. Best for engaged subscribers.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs B
**Reason:** Tests place-anchored ("Hampton Cove") vs stakes-anchored ("None of them showed") openings. Tells you whether your audience opens for local relevance or for narrative tension.

## Preview Text
"$2,400 of deposit on the line. The cleaner showed up at 8 a.m."

## Send Timing
Day 6 — Tuesday at 7:30 AM CT.
Tuesday morning, second hit of the week. The story format reads slower than emails 1–3, so the morning slot gives the reader unhurried time. Story emails get the highest forward rate of any sequence email — make this one shareable.

## Email Copy

Hi {{first_name}},

A story from last fall. Names changed, details didn't.

A renter in Hampton Cove had a deposit walk-through scheduled for Monday morning. $2,400 on the line. She'd called six cleaners over the weekend — three didn't answer, two said they couldn't fit her in until Wednesday, one quoted her $89 and then never sent the confirmation.

She found us on a Saturday night at 9:47 p.m. through Google.

We sent her a flat quote ($425, move-in/out scope, 1,800 square feet, two pets) at 7:14 a.m. Sunday. She accepted at 7:21. We had a team at her door at 8:03 a.m. Monday — a 2-hour arrival window, hit at the front edge.

The crew sent her photos after every room. Inside the oven. Inside the cabinets. Behind the toilets. Baseboards in the empty bedrooms.

The walk-through was at 11. The landlord released the full deposit on the spot.

She emailed us that afternoon: *"I want to write a review but I don't want anyone else to know about you because I'm afraid you'll get too busy."*

(She wrote the review anyway. It's on Google.)

The point of this email isn't the story. The point is what the story shows up against:

- **Six cleaners couldn't return a call on a weekend.** Most don't have weekend coverage. We do.
- **A "starting at $89" cleaner ghosted her.** That price tag was the problem (see email 3).
- **A 2-hour arrival window in writing kept her sane on the night before.** Not "we'll be there sometime Monday."
- **Photos of the finished clean meant she didn't have to be in the empty house to verify.** The photos were the proof.

If you're heading toward a deposit walk-through, a listing showing, or any deadline where the house has to be done by a specific hour — those four things are what to look for in whoever you hire.
