# Email 7: Direct Pitch

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Last note about cleaning, then I'll stop" — recommended
Pattern interrupt + relief signal. "Then I'll stop" lowers the unsubscribe rate because it tells the reader the heavy emails are ending. Highest open of the close emails.

### B: "Yes, no, or not now?"
Three-option directness. Highest reply rate — even "no" replies are useful data. Slightly polarizing — some readers will skip because it feels like a yes/no quiz.

### C: "quick question and then back to regular emails"
Lowercase, conversational. Signals the close to a friend. Lowest unsubscribe of the three.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs C
**Reason:** Tests "I'll stop" relief signal vs "back to regular" continuity signal. The winner tells you whether your audience prefers explicit endings or smooth handoffs to ongoing communication.

## Preview Text
"Whichever answer, I'd love to hear it."

## Send Timing
Day 12 — Thursday at 7:30 AM CT.
Day 12 is the close. Thursday morning gives readers Friday to act if they decide to book; weekend won't pass before the request is on the team's radar. After this email, transitions into the standing newsletter cadence (1 email/2 weeks max).
