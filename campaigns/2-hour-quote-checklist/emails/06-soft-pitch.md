# Email 6: Soft Pitch

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "The actual cost of one-off cleans (do the math)" — recommended
Specificity + invitation to engage. "Do the math" is a confident frame that doesn't push. Matches voice-profile "plainly honest."

### B: "$3,120 vs $2,184 — same year, same house"
Numbers-led, contrarian setup. Highest opens but the dollar figures need to hold up in the email body or you lose trust.

### C: "honest math on what cleaning actually costs"
Lowercase, "honest" as a frame. Best for warm subscribers — implies you're sharing something the industry doesn't want them to see.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs B
**Reason:** Tests qualitative framing ("the actual cost") vs quantitative shock ("$3,120 vs $2,184"). The numbers version usually wins opens but loses some clicks if readers feel manipulated. Tells you how much "math marketing" your audience tolerates.

## Preview Text
"We did the math on what you're spending. The number is bigger than you think."

## Send Timing
Day 10 — Tuesday at 7:30 AM CT.
Day 10 is the first soft pitch. Tuesday morning catches readers at their most decisive point of the week — they're planning, not just consuming. The math format works because it's analytical, not emotional, which lowers the perceived "sales pitch" feel.

I promised math, so here's math. Real numbers from our average bi-weekly customer (3-bed, 2-bath, around 2,000 square feet, no pets):

**Path A — One-off cleans on demand**
- 1 deep clean to reset (every 6 months): $260 × 2 = $520
- 8 maintenance cleans through the year (called when stressed, paid full price): $260 × 8 = $2,080
- Time spent calling, scheduling, and re-vetting: ~12 hours/year
- The Saturday-morning text saying "the cleaner can't make it today": ~3 times/year
- **Total: $2,600/year, roughly 12 hours of your time, plus the chaos**

**Path B — Bi-weekly recurring with the same team**
- 26 cleans/year at the bi-weekly rate (15% off): $221 × 26 = $5,746/year — *but* this isn't an apples-to-apples comparison. You're getting 26 cleans, not 8.
- Per-clean comparison: bi-weekly costs $221 vs one-off at $260. **You save $39 every visit.**
- For roughly the same coverage (18 cleans/year): **$3,978 vs $4,680 on the one-off plan = $702/year saved**
- Time spent calling and scheduling: ~30 minutes/year (just to reschedule the holidays)
- The Saturday-morning "they cancelled" text: 0

The honest version: recurring isn't cheaper if you're trying to use it as the same number of cleans you'd do one-off. It's cheaper *per clean*, and it's dramatically better for keeping the house consistently in shape (because deep work doesn't pile up between visits).

Most of our customers find bi-weekly is the right fit. A few do weekly (small kids, a lot of pets, work-from-home). Some do monthly (empty-nesters, low-traffic homes).
