Here's the thing about the checklist.

It's good. It works. We've gotten emails from people who used it on us, on Molly Maid, on a guy who answered a Nextdoor post — and the right cleaner usually shows up on the other side of it.

But the checklist solves the first-time problem. It doesn't solve the actual problem.

The actual problem is that hiring a cleaner once is a hassle, and hiring a cleaner twice is the same hassle, and hiring a cleaner every time you need one is a lifetime of weekend calls and 5-day quote runarounds and "let me check with my husband and call you back."

The fix isn't a better checklist. The fix is not having to use one.

That's what recurring service is. You vet once — using the checklist or not — and then you stop thinking about it. The same team shows up on the same schedule, your home gets cleaner over time (because the deep work is done and they're just maintaining), and the price drops because you're locked into a recurring rate.

Our recurring rates:

  • Weekly: 20% off the standard rate
  • Bi-weekly: 15% off
  • Monthly: 10% off

Most of our customers land on bi-weekly. It's the price-to-cleanliness sweet spot for a working household with kids or pets.

I'm not asking you to book recurring today. I'm asking you to notice that the checklist solves the wrong problem long-term. The right problem to solve is "how do I never have to think about this again."
