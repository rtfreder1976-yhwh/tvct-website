# Email 2: Connection

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Why we built this checklist" — recommended
Curiosity gap with low friction. Reader expects a story; the implicit promise is they'll learn something about the people behind the business. Matches voice-profile "steady, not hyped."

### B: "We started this business after one bad cleaning"
Bold play. Specific, slightly provocative, opens with the pain. Polarizing — opens fast or gets ignored. Higher unsubscribe risk on a brand-new list.

### C: "quick story about Charlotte, a 4-year-old, and a checklist"
Personal, lowercase, three concrete nouns. Maximally human. Best for engaged subscribers who replied to email 1.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs B
**Reason:** Tests curiosity-led ("Why we built this") vs pain-led ("after one bad cleaning"). Reveals whether your audience opens for context or for stakes — informs the emotional frame for emails 5–7.

Tuesday morning is the highest-engagement window for B2C audiences in the South-East. 7:30 catches the pre-work coffee scan. Avoid Monday (inbox clearing) and avoid weekends (mixed performance for service businesses).

## Email Copy

Hi {{first_name}},

Quick story about why this checklist exists.

Before we started The Valley Clean Team, I (Christen) was the customer. Three different times, in three different houses, I tried to hire someone to clean — and three times I either got ghosted, got a "starting at" quote that turned into something three times higher on the day, or had a stranger walk through my house in a marked car making a face at the laundry.

Todd is a Marine. He runs everything in our life on a checklist. So when we decided to start the business, the first thing we built wasn't a logo. It was a 49-point list of what a clean is supposed to look like, and a process for getting a real flat price to the customer in under 2 hours.

We're a husband-and-wife team, raising a 4-year-old named Charlotte (who has very strong opinions about which cleaning supplies smell best), running this business to the glory of God, and serving five markets across North Alabama and Middle Tennessee.

The checklist you downloaded is the rubric I wish someone had handed me back when I was the frustrated customer. It's free, and it works on whoever you call — including us.

In a couple of days I'll send you the one item from the checklist that gets people the biggest savings. It's the question almost nobody thinks to ask. And it has nothing to do with the price.

If you have a "the worst cleaning I ever paid for" story, hit reply and tell me. I love hearing them — partly because they remind me why we do this, and partly because I learn from every one.

— Christen
Co-owner, The Valley Clean Team
