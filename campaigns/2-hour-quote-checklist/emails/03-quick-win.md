## Email Copy

Hi {{first_name}},

If you've ever called around for cleaning quotes, you've heard this:

> "It's *starting at* $X."

That phrase — "starting at" — is the single most expensive thing a cleaning company says. It almost always means the real number is going to be 30 to 80% higher when the cleaner shows up. We've seen quotes start at $99 and end at $260 on the same day.

Here's the question that fixes it. Word for word:

> "Can you give me a flat, all-in price for that, sent to me in writing?"

Three things to notice:

1. **"Flat, all-in"** — closes the door on add-ons. They can't tack on supplies, travel, or "extra time" later if you anchored to all-in.
2. **"Sent to me in writing"** — a text counts. A voice promise doesn't. If it isn't in writing, it didn't happen.
3. **"For that"** — refers to the specific scope you described (size, condition, deal-breaker rooms). Don't let them re-quote on a vaguer scope.

A real cleaner will answer in under a minute: "Yes — $X, here's a text confirming."

A bait-and-switch cleaner will hedge: "Well, every home is different..." or "We'll have to see when we get there..." That's your signal to keep calling.

Try it on your next quote. Even if it isn't with us. (Especially if it isn't with us — the checklist is the point, not the sales pitch.)

For reference, here are our flat starting numbers across all five markets:

- **Recurring** (weekly / biweekly / monthly): from $99
- **Deep clean** (first visit, post-renovation, 90+ days): from $175
- **Move-in / move-out**: from $225

Final price depends on size and condition — but it's flat, in writing, and the same number on the day.

In a couple of days I'll send you the second-most-expensive thing a cleaning company says — and how to spot it before you book.

— Todd
Co-owner, The Valley Clean Team
