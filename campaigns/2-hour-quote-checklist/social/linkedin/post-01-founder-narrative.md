## Post

A small business lesson from running a cleaning company across five markets.

The way most cleaning gets sold in this country is broken.

A homeowner submits a form. It takes 2 days for a callback. A marked vehicle pulls up to walk the house. Then "starting at $X" lands in the inbox — a number that almost always becomes 30 to 80% higher when the cleaner shows up.

Three years ago my wife Christen and I started The Valley Clean Team and decided to invert all of that:

→ Quote in under 2 hours, not 5 days
→ Flat price, not "starting at"
→ Same-day arrival window, in writing
→ Same team every visit
→ Photo of every finished room

None of those things are technically hard. They're just operationally inconvenient. They require a dispatch system, a pricing model that holds up without a site visit, and a labor structure that keeps the same crew on the same accounts.

We built all of that — and our customer acquisition cost dropped meaningfully. Turns out that when you remove the friction the rest of the industry has built into the buying process, you don't need to spend as much convincing someone to buy.

I'm sharing this not as a brag but as a reminder for anyone running a service business: the standard playbook in your category is a list of inconveniences your customers have learned to tolerate. The ones you remove become the basis of your positioning.

We wrote the operational playbook into a free 7-question checklist that any homeowner can use to vet any cleaner — including ours. Link in the comments if useful for anyone in our service area.
