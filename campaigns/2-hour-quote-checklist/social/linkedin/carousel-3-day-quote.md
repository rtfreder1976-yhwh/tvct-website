# LinkedIn Carousel — "Why your cleaner takes 5 days to quote you"

## Why this works
LinkedIn carousels in 2026 = highest dwell time of any post format on the platform. Dwell time is the #1 ranking signal. Made for scroll-stop-then-tap-tap-tap consumption. Reuses the "broken process" frame from the Facebook post but turns it into a business-thinking story rather than a homeowner pitch.

---

## Slide content

### SLIDE 1 — Cover
```
Why your cleaner
takes 5 days
to quote you.

(And what we did differently
when we built ours.)

— Todd Freder
   The Valley Clean Team
```

### SLIDE 2 — The standard process
```
The standard cleaning quote
in this country:

Day 1: Form submission
Day 2: Callback
Day 3: In-home estimate
Day 4: "Starting at $X" email
Day 5: Booking
```

### SLIDE 3 — The cost of the standard
```
Two costs hide inside that
5-day process:

→ The customer's cost:
   they needed it clean today,
   not next week

→ The business's cost:
   ~50% of warm leads go cold
   before booking
```

### SLIDE 4 — The inversion
```
What if you ran it the
other direction?

Quote in under 2 hours.
Flat price (no "starting at").
Same-day arrival window.
In writing.
```

### SLIDE 5 — Why nobody does it
```
None of those things are hard.

They're operationally inconvenient.

They require:
→ Dispatch infrastructure
→ Pricing models that hold up
   without a site visit
→ Labor structure that keeps
   the same crew on accounts
```

### SLIDE 6 — What it costs to build
```
Building it took us
about a year.

Three full hires earlier
than we planned.

A scheduling system rewrite.

A flat-pricing model that
leaves margin on tail-risk
jobs but holds on the median.
```

### SLIDE 7 — What it returned
```
What changed:

→ CAC dropped meaningfully
→ Conversion rate ~3x on
   urgency-driven inbound
→ Lifetime value held
   (recurring rate steady)
→ Reviews shifted from
   "good service" to
   "I can't believe they
    showed up that fast"
```

### SLIDE 8 — CTA
```
The lesson:

The standard playbook in
your category is a list of
inconveniences customers
have learned to tolerate.

The ones you remove become
the basis of your positioning.

— Todd Freder
   The Valley Clean Team
```

This carousel is what we learned about why nobody else has done it — and what it actually cost us to build.

Useful for any service-business operator looking at their own category and wondering which of the "industry standard" frictions are actually load-bearing.
