## Post copy

A small rant about cleaning pricing.

If a cleaner ever quotes you "starting at $X," that price is almost never the price you'll pay. We've watched quotes start at $99 and end at $260 on the same day, on the same house.

"Starting at" is industry-speak for "we'll figure out the rest when we're standing in your kitchen." Supplies, extra. Travel, extra. "It needed extra time," extra. Pets, extra. Two-story foyer, extra.

There's a question that kills it. Word for word:

→ "Can you give me a flat, all-in price for that, sent to me in writing?"

Three things to notice:

→ "Flat, all-in" closes the door on add-ons.
→ "Sent to me in writing" — a text counts. Voice promises don't.
→ "For that" pins them to the scope you described.

A real cleaner answers in under a minute. A bait-and-switch cleaner hedges. That hedge is your signal to keep calling.

We charge flat. Always have. For reference:

→ Recurring (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly) — from $99
→ Deep clean — from $175
→ Move-in/out — from $225

Final price depends on size and condition. But it's flat. In writing. The same number on the day.

If you've been burned by a "starting at" quote — drop it in the comments. We collect these. The patterns are unbelievable.
