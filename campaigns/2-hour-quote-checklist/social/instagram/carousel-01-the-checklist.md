```
The way most cleaning
gets sold is broken:

→ Day 1, you fill out a form
→ Day 3, someone walks your house
→ Day 5, "starting at $X" lands
```

```
1.

Decide what kind of clean
you actually need.

Recurring. Deep. Move-in/out.
Three categories, three prices.

If a cleaner can't tell which
fits without an in-home visit —
they're guessing.
```

### SLIDE 4 — Item 4 (the speed test)
```
4.

Time the response.

A real local team replies
in under 2 hours.

Most chains take 24–72 hours.
Aggregator apps reply fast
with a different stranger
every visit.
```

### SLIDE 5 — Item 6 (the arrival window)
```
6.

Get the arrival window
in writing.

A real same-day job comes
with a 2–4 hour window,
sent by text.

"We'll try to fit you in"
isn't same-day.
```

### SLIDE 6 — Item 7 (the conversion lever)
```
7.

Ask for a flat price.
Not "starting at."

"Starting at $X" almost
always becomes 30–80%
more on the day.

A flat number in writing
is the price you'll pay.
```

### SLIDE 7 — The 3 vetting questions
```
3 bonus questions
that reveal a serious cleaner:

→ Are you insured?
   For how much, in dollars?

→ Is it the same team
   every time?

→ Do I get photos
   of the finished clean?
```
