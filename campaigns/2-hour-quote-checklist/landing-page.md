Here's how the average cleaning quote actually goes:

- **Day 1:** You fill out a form. You wait.
- **Day 2:** Someone calls back. They want to schedule an in-home estimate.

- **Day 4:** You get an email. The price says "starting at $X" — which is not the price you'll actually pay.

Total time: 5 days. Total time you actually had: 2.

That's the broken process. The chains do it because it's their playbook. The aggregator apps (Homeaglow, Handy, Care.com) skip it but send you a different uninsured stranger every visit.

## A/B TESTS TO RUN

```
Test 1: Headline — Direct benefit (#1) vs Proof-led ("1,000+ Valley homeowners...")
Why:     Cold traffic vs warm traffic respond differently. Direct hits the
         skeptical first-timer; proof-led hits the comparison shopper.
Metric:  Opt-in rate (form submits / page visits)
Impact:  +15–30% on the better-fit audience
Priority: HIGH — segment your traffic and run both

Test 2: CTA copy — "Send me the checklist" vs "Get the free checklist"
Why:     "Send me" is reader-voiced (less commanding); "Get the free" emphasizes price.
Metric:  CTA click rate
Impact:  +5–15%
Priority: HIGH — single-click decision

Test 3: Form fields — 2 required (name, email) vs 3 (add ZIP)
Why:     ZIP enables market-specific welcome emails but adds friction.
Metric:  Opt-in rate vs welcome-sequence conversion
Impact:  -5 to -10% on opt-in, +10 to +20% on subsequent booking conversion
Priority: MEDIUM — only run after baseline conversion is steady

Test 4: Trust signals — Lead with "$2M insured + 130+ reviews" vs "Veteran-owned, women-led"
Why:     The first is rational proof; the second is identity proof. Different
         buyers respond to each. CRITICAL: do NOT use 49-point checklist or
         "veteran + women-owned" as the primary headline in Huntsville/Shoals
         (MaidPro collision per brand/positioning.md).
Metric:  Opt-in rate by market
Impact:  Up to +20% in identity-resonant markets (Mountain Brook, Nashville)
         Up to -5% if used as headline in MaidPro markets (Huntsville/Shoals)
Priority: MEDIUM — segment by market

Test 5: Confirmation page CTA — Phone numbers prominent vs subdued
Why:     The hottest leads on this list are urgency-driven and may convert
         on the confirmation page itself, before the email even arrives.
Metric:  Inbound calls/texts within 30 min of opt-in
Impact:  Material — these are the highest-intent leads on the list
Priority: HIGH — easy win
```
