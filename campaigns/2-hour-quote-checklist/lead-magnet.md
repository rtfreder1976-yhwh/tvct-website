# The 2-Hour Quote Checklist
## 7 things to know before you book any cleaner — so you get a real price today, not a sales call on Thursday

## Phase 1: Before You Call

### ☐ 1. Decide what kind of clean you actually need

Cleaners price three things very differently. Knowing which one you need cuts your quote time in half.

- **Recurring** — every week, every two weeks, or once a month. Lowest per-visit price (often 15–20% off the deep-clean rate).
- **Deep clean** — for first visits, post-renovation, or any home that hasn't been professionally cleaned in 90+ days. Higher price, longer time on site.
- **Move-in / move-out** — empty home, focus on appliances, baseboards, inside cabinets. Priced separately because the scope is different.

**Quick tip:** If a cleaner won't tell you which category you fall into without a visit, that's a red flag. A real one can usually tell from a photo or a square-footage number.

### ☐ 2. Write down your bedroom and bathroom count

Almost every legitimate cleaning quote uses bedrooms + bathrooms as the primary input. If you're calling, have it ready: "3 bed, 2 bath, 2,200 square feet."

**Quick tip:** Half-baths count as half a bath. Most pricing rounds them up. Be honest — under-counting now means a surprise add-on later.

### ☐ 3. Identify the deal-breaker rooms

Some rooms add real time and should be flagged before you get a quote:

- A separate primary bath with a soaker tub
- A finished basement with a second kitchen
- Two-story foyers or staircases needing a ladder
- Pet areas (litter, crates, rugs)
- A home office with electronics

**Quick tip:** Mention these up front. A cleaner who doesn't ask about them will either upcharge you on the day or rush through them. Both are bad.

## Phase 2: When You Call (or Text, or Click)

### ☐ 4. Time the response

Open your phone clock when you submit the form, send the text, or hang up. **A real local team responds in under 2 hours during business hours.** Most chains take 24–72 hours. Aggregator apps (Homeaglow, Handy, Care.com) respond fast but match you with a different gig worker every time.

**Quick tip:** If they take longer than two hours to respond to a quote request, ask yourself — how long will they take to respond when something goes wrong on a clean?

### ☐ 5. Have your square footage ready

Look it up on Zillow, your closing docs, or the county property records — it takes 30 seconds. Without it, every cleaner will pad the quote to protect themselves.

**Quick tip:** Don't include garage square footage. Most cleaners don't service it.

### ☐ 6. Get the arrival window in writing

A real same-day commitment is a window, not a vague "later today." For us it's a 2–4 hour arrival window after you accept the quote, sent to you by text. Anyone who says "we'll try to fit you in" is not actually offering same-day service.

**Quick tip:** Screenshot it. If they push back on putting an arrival window in a text, they don't really do same-day.

### ☐ 7. Ask for a flat price, not "starting at"

This is the single most important item on this list. The phrase "starting at $X" means "you will pay more than $X." A flat price means the number you saw is the number you pay.

A real flat quote includes:
- Labor for the entire clean
- Supplies and equipment
- Travel within the service area
- All the rooms you discussed

It does **not** include surprise "we needed extra time" add-ons. If a cleaner won't give you a flat number, ask why. The honest answer ("we haven't seen the home") tells you they need a visit before quoting — fine, but it's not same-day. The dishonest answer ("our prices vary") tells you to keep calling.

**Quick tip:** If you get a flat quote, get it in writing before they show up. A text with the price + arrival window is enough.

## Bonus: Three questions that reveal a serious cleaner

Once you have a quote, three more questions separate the pros from the rest:

1. **"Are you insured? For how much?"** — A serious cleaner answers in dollars. Ours is $2 million. If they pause, they're either uninsured or under-insured. Either way, your homeowner's policy may not cover damage caused by an uninsured contractor.

2. **"Will the same team come every time?"** — Aggregator apps and most large chains rotate cleaners. That means a stranger in your home every visit and inconsistent quality. A real local team sends the same crew so they learn your home.

3. **"Do I get photos of the finished clean?"** — Photo-documented cleans are still rare. They're proof you got what you paid for, and they give you something concrete if anything is missed.
