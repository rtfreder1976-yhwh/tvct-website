# Campaign: The 2-Hour Quote Checklist

## Goal
Build TVCT's email list with high-intent, urgency-driven cleaning buyers across all five markets, then convert them into first-time bookings (deep clean, move-in/out) or recurring service subscribers. Target: ≥10% landing-page opt-in rate, ≥15% lead-to-booked-quote within the welcome sequence.

## Format
Checklist (7 numbered items + 3 bonus questions, ~5 min consume / 10 min apply)

## Hook
"The 2-Hour Quote Checklist: 7 things to know before you book any cleaner — so you get a real price today, not a sales call on Thursday"

## Target Audience
Urgency-driven cleaning buyers across Huntsville, Athens, Florence/Shoals, Birmingham, Mountain Brook, and Nashville. Sub-segments:
- First-time professional-cleaning hirers
- Last-minute hosts (in-laws Friday, party Saturday, listing showing tomorrow)
- Renters facing a deposit walk-through
- Busy parents and dual-career households who can't take a Tuesday off for an in-home estimate
Awareness level: problem-aware, comparing options. Sophistication: low-to-medium — most have either never hired pros or have been burned by Homeaglow/Handy.

## Bridge to Paid Offer
The checklist itself benchmarks what a "real" cleaning quote looks like (flat price, 2-hour response, same-day arrival window, $2M insurance, photo proof, same team every visit). Each criterion is a lever competitors fail and TVCT meets. By the final item, the reader has built a mental rubric that points to TVCT — so the closing CTA ("if nobody else passes, we do") is the natural next step, not a sales jump.

## Paid Offer
TVCT same-day/next-day cleaning across:
- Recurring (weekly / biweekly / monthly) — highest LTV target
- Deep clean (first-time, post-construction, post-event)
- Move-in / move-out
Price entry points: deep cleans from $276; recurring with up to 20% discount.

## Competitive Differentiation
- **MaidPro / Molly Maid / Merry Maids** require multi-day estimates. Checklist makes that delay visible.
- **Homeaglow / Handy / Care.com** offer same-day but no insurance, no consistent team, no photo proof. Checklist's "3 bonus questions" surface those gaps.
- No major competitor is publishing a "how to vet a cleaner" educational asset for this region. First-mover on the format.
- Avoids the MaidPro collision: does NOT lead with "veteran + women-owned" or "49-point checklist" headline (those are MaidPro's claims in Huntsville/Shoals too). Trust signals appear at the end as supporting proof, not as the hook.

## Distribution Plan
1. **Landing page** — dedicated opt-in page, written next via /direct-response-copy. CTAs on homepage hero, service pages, location pages.
2. **Site-wide exit-intent / scroll-trigger popup** — fires on services and location pages.
3. **Sticky in-content CTA** — added to all blog posts, especially high-traffic SEO pages.
4. **Same-day landing pages** — Athens and Nashville same-day pages already exist; pin checklist as the secondary CTA after "call now."
5. **Local Service Ads / Google Ads** — use the checklist as the conversion offer for non-call-clickers.
6. **Social** — atomized via /content-atomizer for Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn (Mountain Brook + Nashville lean), Nextdoor (community-trust angle).
7. **Email signature** of Todd, Christen, and the team.

## Voice Notes
Per `brand/voice-profile.md`:
- Steady, not hyped — zero exclamation points in the checklist body. One soft sign-off CTA, no shouting.
- Disciplined warmth — concrete numbers throughout ($2M, 2 hours, 2–4 hour window, 3 bed/2 bath).
- Plainly honest — pricing uses "From $276" pattern; we explicitly tell the reader to book whoever passes the checklist, not just us. Trust comes from being willing to lose them.
- Neighbor-first — names the five markets explicitly; "Todd & Christen" sign-off.
- Vocabulary: "same team, every time," "$2M insured," "no surprises," avoided "amazing/sparkle/best/luxury."
- Faith/family thread held back from the checklist itself (wrong moment) — surface it in the welcome email sequence instead, where the relationship has started.
